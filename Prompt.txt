Q: Why do we use a 2D array canvas[ROWS][COLS]?

A: It stores the picture. Each element represents one position on the screen and contains either _ or *.

2. Q: What is the purpose of initCanvas()?

A: It fills the entire canvas with _ to create a blank drawing area.

3. Q: Why is displayCanvas() used?

A: It prints the current contents of the canvas on the screen.

4. Q: What does the plot() function do?

A: It places a * at the given coordinate (x,y) if it is inside the canvas boundary.

5. Q: Why do we check boundaries in plot()?

A: To prevent accessing invalid array locations and avoid errors.

if(x >= 0 && x < ROWS && y >= 0 && y < COLS)
6. Q: What is the logic of drawLine()?

A: It plots points from the start coordinate to the end coordinate one by one, forming a line.

7. Q: Why is abs() used in drawLine()?

A: abs() returns the positive value of a number.

Example:

abs(-5) = 5

Used to calculate distance differences:

dx = abs(x2 - x1);
dy = abs(y2 - y1);
8. Q: What are dx and dy?

A:

dx = horizontal distance
dy = vertical distance

Example:

(2,3) to (8,10)

dx = 6
dy = 7
9. Q: What are sx and sy?

A: They determine the direction of movement.

sx = 1  -> move down/right
sx = -1 -> move up/left

Same for sy.

10. Q: How is a rectangle drawn?

A: By drawing four lines:

Top
Bottom
Left

drawLine(...)
11. Q: Why do we use width and height for a rectangle?

A: Width gives horizontal size and height gives vertical size.

12. Q: How is a circle drawn?

A: Using angle values from 0° to 359°.

For each angle:

x = xc + r*cos(angle)
y = yc + r*sin(angle)

Then a point is plotted.

13. Q: What are xc and yc?

A: They are the center coordinates of the circle.

14. Q: Why is cos() used?

A: It calculates the horizontal position of a point on the circle.

15. Q: Why is sin() used?

A: It calculates the vertical position of a point on the circle.

16. Q: Why convert degrees to radians?

A: sin() and cos() work with radians.

rad = angle * 3.14159 / 180;
17. Q: How is a triangle drawn?

A: By drawing three lines between three vertices.

drawLine(x1,y1,x2,y2);
drawLine(x2,y2,x3,y3);
drawLine(x3,y3,x1,y1);
18. Q: What is the purpose of deleteArea()?

A: It removes drawings from a selected rectangular area by replacing * with _.

19. Q: Why use nested loops in deleteArea()?

A: To visit every row and column inside the selected area.

20. Q: What happens when choice 7 is selected?

A: The canvas is cleared by calling:

A: Functions make the program modular, reusable, and easier to understand.
